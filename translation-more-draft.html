Hebrew to English Translations

The translation process:

Before the beginning of a project, requirements are agreed upon in writing,
with details such as rounds of clarification questions (usually one), deadlines and payment details, so that you can be sure you will stay on schedule and within budget.

I start with a literal translation.

Every word in the original source text has its meaning appear in the translation. Additionally, I use a method where the entire text is divided into small portions, Hebrew and English next to each other, so that both the translator and the client can easily focus on each written sentence.

I choose each translated word carefully.

Because English has many more words than Hebrew, often there are numerous choices in English for one word in Hebrew. That variety helps us because, although in Hebrew, words from the same root are commonly used in one sentence, in English, it's poor writing style. We try not to use the same words or words with the same root in close proximity, unless there are no other suitable choices. If there is any question of intention in an original text, it's important that the author and the translator are in contact. It is our joint goal that the translation follows the original in meaning as closely as possible.
